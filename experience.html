About Me

I'm from Boulder, which means I grew up on bikes before I cared about textbooks. Coached mountain bike camps in high school, picked up Cal Poly for the hands-on EE program, and generally do better when I'm making something than when I'm reading about it.

Now in my first year of EE. The coursework is good. The lab time is better.

What I'm Into

Mountain Biking

Been riding trails around Boulder my whole life. Coached camps for two summers — groups of up to 15 middle schoolers, full days in the mountains. You learn fast that good plans are great, but adaptability is what actually keeps things moving. Still ride whenever I'm back home.

Building Things

My side projects and my coursework end up looking a lot alike. Both start with a question I can't answer without building something and checking. The reaction time tester came from actually wanting to know the answer, not from an assignment.

Big Questions

Summer before college I did a residential program at Columbia — Engineering and Astrophysics. Mostly went because the scale of the questions was interesting. Came back with a better sense of how to think about problems I don't know how to solve yet.
